## Executive summary

A **camera ablation study** was run on the step-3000 best checkpoint: baseline (top + wrist) vs **top camera zeroed** (wrist only). Teacher-forcing val loss **regresses +15%** without the top view (0.388 → 0.446), while open-loop action RMSE is **unchanged within noise** (0.119 → 0.116). The model clearly uses the top camera for flow-matching during teacher forcing, but wrist-only predictions remain similarly accurate on this single held-out episode. **Deploy with both cameras**; do not treat wrist-only offline RMSE as evidence that top is dispensable on-robot.

## Interpretation (paper-grounded)

MolmoAct2 (Fang et al., arXiv:2605.02881) is designed for **multi-view spatial reasoning** — top-down scene context plus wrist egocentric detail. Zeroing the top camera removes global layout cues (panel location, base, approach geometry) while preserving end-effector-relative information in the wrist stream.

The **+15% teacher-forcing loss regression** under `no_top` indicates the flow-matching action expert was trained to condition on both views; removing top increases denoising error even when ground-truth actions are available at eval time. That is the expected signature of a view the model actually uses.

The **flat or slightly better open-loop RMSE** without top is less intuitive but plausible on this setup: (1) only **one val episode** — deltas below ~0.005 RMSE are within run noise; (2) open-loop scoring compares predicted chunks to recorded actions frame-by-frame, which can decouple from teacher-forcing loss when the VLM prompt still includes a black top image slot; (3) reach-heavy joints (2–4) show mixed direction, not a clean “top helps reach” story offline.

**Joint 4** remains the largest error under both conditions (~0.039 MSE), consistent with the prior eval — brush approach dominates, and offline metrics still cannot validate contact.

---

## Recommendations

1. **Always run inference with both cameras** on-robot; the ablation shows top view is load-bearing for the flow head even if open-loop RMSE looks similar.
2. **Repeat ablation on-robot** (5–10 trials per condition) — offline wrist-only RMSE is not sufficient to drop a camera in deployment.
3. **Optional:** run `--ablation-study` with `--ablate-camera wrist` to test whether wrist or top contributes more to joint 4 errors.
4. Continue **collect more demonstrations** (40–100 episodes) before drawing strong multi-camera conclusions; N=1 val episode limits all ablation claims.
5. Keep **best checkpoint by training eval loss**; ablation does not change checkpoint selection.

---

## Advisor conclusion

**Recommendation:** **Proceed to robot eval with both cameras**, and treat this ablation as **supporting evidence that top view matters for the flow expert**, not as license to run wrist-only.

Teacher-forcing loss clearly degrades without top; open-loop metrics on a single episode are too noisy to override that signal. Confidence in ablation conclusions would increase materially with more val episodes and physical trials. If on-robot wrist-only trials succeed unexpectedly, revisit whether top can be dropped for latency — until then, keep the full camera stack.
