## Interpretation (paper-grounded)

MolmoAct2 (Fang et al., arXiv:2605.02881) couples a frozen or lightly adapted VLM to a **flow-matching continuous action expert** trained on embodiment-specific data. This run used **action-expert-only** fine-tuning on only **nine training episodes** — orders of magnitude smaller than MolmoAct2-SO100/101 pretraining mixtures. Under that regime, teacher-forcing eval loss near 0.35–0.38 is plausible: the model fits local demonstration kinematics but val variance is dominated by a **single held-out episode**.

The gap between training best eval (**0.356**) and offline eval (**0.377**, +5.9%) is modest and likely reflects batching, full-episode open-loop roll-through, or slight train/eval pipeline differences — not necessarily degradation.

Open-loop RMSE **0.118** in normalized action space is more deployment-relevant than teacher-forcing loss. **Joint 4** (highest MSE) is consistent with reach-dominated motion during brush approach; MolmoAct’s emphasis on multi-view spatial reasoning suggests verifying wrist-camera coverage during that phase before blaming capacity limits.

---

## Recommendations

1. **Run 10–20 on-robot trials** on brush + return-to-base; offline metrics cannot validate contact or recovery.
2. **Collect 40–100 more episodes** before architectural changes; data volume is the primary gap vs. MolmoAct2 paper setups.
3. **Inspect joint 4 failures** in val episode video — if errors cluster at reach-to-panel, add demos with varied approach angles.
4. If robot trials stall while train loss keeps falling, retry with **`ENABLE_LORA_VLM=True`** at lower ViT LR (per PLAN.md).
5. Keep deploying **`best` checkpoint by eval loss**, not step 3000 by default if a lower-eval checkpoint appears in future runs.

## Advisor conclusion

**Recommendation:** **Proceed to robot eval** (limited trials), with **collect more demonstrations** as the parallel priority.

The checkpoint shows coherent flow-matching behavior on held-out frames and reasonable open-loop errors, but the experimental N is too small for strong generalization claims. Success on the physical brush task — especially contact consistency and return-to-base — should override offline loss as the decision criterion. If on-robot success is below ~50% on simple repeats, prioritize data over hyperparameter sweeps.
